ATmega8 Prototyping PCB

 HOME > Creative Technology > AVR Mega8 PCB

  • Make the PCB with Toner Transfer
  • Use an Atmel AVR chip, the ATmega8
  • Enough room for a ULN2803 darlington driver chip (18-pin DIL)
  • Use up a spare piece of copper-clad board I've had since the 1970s
  • Maximise the usefulness of the PCB for breadboarding/prototyping
  • Enough room for two 16-pin DIL chips in addition to AVR chip
  • Enough room to expand I/O pins of AVR with shift registers, muxes, etc.
  • Six-pin programming header, but with polarisation keyway
  • Reset button
  • PCB tracks wide enough for easy toner transfer
  • PCB layout for four TO220 power transistors or MOSFETs
  • Power-on LED
  • Minimise area of copper etched away, to make ferric chloride last longer
  • Stick to business card or ISO credit card size
  • Ground track all around outside edges
  • Use free and open source tools, e.g. GNU PCB

More photos of the PCB and the etching process are in this Flickr set.
